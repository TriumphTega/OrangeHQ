Chapter 5

Jared, unlike Denn, was a fine representation of what the Rumi bloodline looked like.

Tall, with dark wavy hair and the sea-green eyes that was characteristic of their family's children, the boy stood at just over six feet, compared to Denn's scrawny little shape.

And for all of his height and heft, not one single inch of the bigger boy was a soft spot for the smaller one. Denn did not know why, but Jared absolutely hated his very existence. Facing him on the sands was always a nightmare, because he never pulled his blows, or went easy when using his powers.

The view from deep within the pits covered the ocean, because the width of the fighting arenas could be said to rather fit the pits being called the caverns.

The walls had the usual jaunts were people in the droves came to watch. And with the ocean sparkling a green hue beyond them was spectacular as always; the setting sun liked to bath everything in a warm, and ethereal orange glow.

This was a place called Orange.

A few of the returning fishing vessels were silhouetted against the glittering water, making their way tranquilly into harbor at the end of a long day.

And yet. This was the time the ilumi beat their drums.

Perhaps it was the way the water possessed a heat in this time, but the people's prayers included flocking to the vast piers that stood for the docks, to drink their fill of the luminous looking water. Everyday.
